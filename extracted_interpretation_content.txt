P37 [normal]: Realistic:
P38 [normal]: Realistic individuals are practical, grounded, and focused on the present moment rather than abstract ideas or distant possibilities. They prefer engaging in hands-on tasks that produce tangible, visible results and enjoy working with tools, machinery, animals, or natural materials. Their thinking is structured and logical, and they tend to rely on proven, reliable methods when solving problems. Realistic people value stability, routine, and clarity, often thriving in environments that have clear goals, well-defined authority, and measurable outcomes. They are typically assertive, competitive, and reserved, preferring action and practicality over excessive discussion or paperwork. Their satisfaction comes from creating, repairing, or building things they can see and touch, often in outdoor or technical settings. Common careers for them include engineer, electrician, pilot, firefighter, carpenter, mechanic, and agricultural or construction worker, where they can see the immediate impact of their efforts.
P40 [normal]: Investigative:
P41 [normal]: Investigative individuals are analytical, curious, and intellectually driven, preferring to explore ideas rather than engage in physical or social activities. They enjoy understanding cause-and-effect relationships, solving complex problems, and using logic and reasoning to uncover facts. Independent and introspective, they thrive in unstructured environments where they can think deeply, research, and experiment without constant supervision. Their interests lie in discovering, collecting, and analyzing data or theories, often within scientific, mathematical, or technical fields. They are original thinkers with strong vocabularies and a desire to classify, understand, and explain phenomena. These individuals are often more comfortable with ideas and systems than with people or routine tasks. They seek intellectual challenge and are motivated by curiosity and discovery. Careers that suit them include psychologist, scientist, engineer, software developer, economist, physician, and researcher. Such roles allow them to apply their intellect and analytical skills to meaningful, knowledge-driven work.
P43 [normal]: Artistic:
P44 [normal]: Artistic individuals are creative, expressive, and guided by imagination and emotion. They are drawn to cultural and aesthetic pursuits, valuing originality, beauty, and self-expression over structure or routine. These individuals often express themselves indirectly through their chosen medium—such as art, music, writing, or performance—and prefer tasks that allow personal creativity and freedom. They enjoy working with ideas, designs, and patterns to create something meaningful or emotionally resonant. Artistic people thrive in flexible, unstructured environments that value innovation and individuality rather than rigid rules or bureaucratic systems. They are often seen as unconventional and may struggle in overly structured or monotonous settings. Their work typically revolves around creating, performing, or communicating ideas that inspire others. They flourish in careers such as writer, artist, musician, designer, actor, editor, choreographer, or filmmaker, where they can bring imagination to life and transform emotions into art.
P46 [normal]: Social:
P47 [normal]: Social individuals are compassionate, empathetic, and people-oriented, preferring to work with others rather than with data, machines, or physical objects. They value connection, communication, and personal growth—both their own and others’. With strong verbal and interpersonal skills, they are effective at teaching, guiding, helping, and inspiring those around them. These individuals are sensitive to others’ needs and find fulfillment in roles that involve care, understanding, and social contribution. They thrive in environments that encourage collaboration, emotional expression, and learning rather than rigid structure or routine. Social people often avoid technical or mechanical tasks, as they prefer meaningful human interaction. Their motivation lies in making a positive difference and supporting others’ development and well-being. They are commonly found in helping professions such as teacher, counsellor, psychologist, nurse, social worker, therapist, or clergy, where empathy, communication, and human connection are central to their work.
P49 [normal]: Enterprising:
P50 [normal]: Enterprising individuals are ambitious, confident, and action-oriented, thriving in dynamic environments where they can lead, persuade, and achieve results. They enjoy working with people and ideas, using their communication and leadership skills to influence, motivate, and direct others. These individuals are often competitive and driven by goals related to success, status, and financial reward. They prefer initiating and managing projects, taking calculated risks, and turning opportunities into achievements. Enterprising people are natural decision-makers who value independence, power, and progress. They tend to avoid routine, detailed, or highly analytical work, as they find greater satisfaction in fast-paced, results-oriented settings. Their energy and assertiveness make them effective in roles that involve promotion, negotiation, or leadership. They often excel in careers such as entrepreneur, manager, lawyer, stockbroker, salesperson, public relations executive, or political leader—fields that reward confidence, initiative, and achievement.
P52 [normal]: Conventional:
P53 [normal]: Conventional individuals are organized, detail-oriented, and efficient, preferring structure, stability, and clear rules in both work and life. They enjoy working with data, numbers, and systems rather than abstract ideas or open-ended creativity. These individuals value order, precision, and routine, finding satisfaction in tasks that involve accuracy, recordkeeping, and following established procedures. They thrive in environments with well-defined authority, predictable routines, and measurable outcomes, where efficiency and reliability are appreciated. Conventional people are dependable and methodical, preferring clarity over ambiguity and tradition over experimentation. They excel in business-like or administrative settings that emphasize consistency and precision. Though they may not seek leadership roles, they play a vital part in maintaining organization and smooth operations. Common careers for them include accountant, auditor, financial analyst, administrative assistant, bookkeeper, banker, or data clerk—roles where structure, responsibility, and attention to detail are key.
